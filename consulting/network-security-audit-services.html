FAQ

Common Questions About Security Audits

Details outlining timelines, operational impact, and required engagement accesses.

How long does a network security audit typically take?
+
Depending on your organization's network complexity, a comprehensive audit typically takes between 2 to 4 weeks traversing from the initial kickoff to final executive reporting.
What kind of access do you need for the security audit?
+
We strictly require temporary, monitored "read-only" access to your key network gateway systems, active directories, and baseline configuration dashboards to conduct analysis dynamically.
Will the audit disrupt our daily operations?
+
No. We perform all necessary network audits in a non-intrusive manner that explicitly guarantees your internal business continuity remains untouched during the assessment.
Do you provide direct remediation support?
+
Yes. Beyond just identifying the risks, we aggressively help you prioritize and physically resolve these security gaps across your systems based on impact severity and effort.
What is the difference between VA and PT?
+
A Vulnerability Assessment (VA) identifies known flaws, while a Penetration Test (PT) actively attempts to exploit them to determine the depth of potential unauthorized access.
How often should we conduct a network audit?
+
We recommend at least one comprehensive annual audit, supplemented by quarterly vulnerability scans or immediate re-audits after significant infrastructure changes.
Can you audit cloud-based network environments?
+
Yes, we perform specialized audits for AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, focusing on cloud-native security groups, IAM policies, and VPC configurations.
What standards do you follow during the audit?
+
Our audits are mapped strictly to international benchmarks such as NIST SP 800-53, CIS Critical Security Controls, and ISO 27001 security standards.
