FAQ

Common Questions About Cybersecurity Services

Clear answers for teams planning their next security upgrade.

What types of cybersecurity services do you provide?
+
We provide security assessments, vulnerability management, penetration testing, SOC monitoring support, endpoint and identity security, network security hardening, cloud security reviews, incident response readiness, and compliance-aligned cybersecurity programs.
Can you support compliance-driven environments?
+
Yes. Our cybersecurity services are often aligned to ISO 27001, PCI DSS, HIPAA, and other governance requirements, helping businesses improve technical controls while preparing for audits and ongoing compliance obligations.
Do you work with both one-time projects and ongoing managed services?
+
Yes. We support focused engagements such as assessments and remediation planning, as well as longer-term cybersecurity partnerships for continuous improvement, monitoring, and advisory support.
How do you approach remediation after a security assessment?
+
We prioritize findings based on business risk, technical impact, and implementation effort. That means you get a clear roadmap with actionable next steps, not just a list of issues.
How do you handle emerging zero-day threats?
+
Our AI-driven SOC uses behavioral analysis and global threat intelligence to detect and neutralize unknown threats in real-time, often before they can be identified by traditional signature-based systems.
Is your security approach compliant with international standards?
+
Yes, we align our strategies with ISO 27001, NIST, and SOC2 frameworks. This ensures your security posture not only protects your assets but also meets global regulatory and industry standards.
Can you help after a security breach has already occurred?
+
Yes, we provide emergency incident response and digital forensics. We help isolate the threat, recover critical data, and implement hardened security controls to prevent any recurrence of the breach.
What is the typical timeline for a penetration test?
+
A standard penetration test usually takes 1 to 2 weeks, depending on the scope of your environment. This includes thorough testing, data analysis, and the delivery of a comprehensive remediation report.
